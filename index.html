The MP Model of the Labor Market

Background

The simulation is based on the transition equation for the unemployment rate: $$u_{t+1} = (1 - A\sqrt{\theta_t} - \lambda) u_t + \lambda,$$ the vacancy supply equation: $$\theta_t = \left[\frac{A}{\kappa}\left( \frac{y-w_t}{\lambda}\right) \right]^2,$$ and wage setting equations: $$w_t = \beta y + (1-\beta) b + \beta \kappa \theta_t.$$ Note that the last two equations imply that \(w_t\) and \(\theta\) are effectively static functions of model parameters and that they are always equal to their steady state values. This means that in response to a one-time permanent change in any of the model parameters, \(w\) and \(\theta\) jump immediately to their new steady state values while the unemployment rate smoothly transitions to its new steady state values. Furthermore, the transition equation for the unemploymet rate determines the steady state relationship between the unemployment rate and market tightness: $$ u = \frac{\lambda}{A\sqrt{\theta} + \lambda} $$ We call this last expression the modified Beveridge curve.
