Revisiting Unemployment in Intermediate Macroeconomics: A New Approach for Teaching Mortensen-Pissarides

About

This page contains resources to accompany the paper Revisiting Unemployment in Intermediate Macroeconomics: A New Approach for Teaching Mortensen-Pissarides by Arghya Bhattacharya, Paul Jackson, and Brian C Jenkins. In our paper, we present a simplified version of the Mortensen and Pissarides (1994) model of unemployment that is accessible to undergraduates and preserves the dynamic structure of the original model. For brevity, we refer presentation of the Mortensen and Pissarides model as the MP model.

Below you will find a link to Beveridge curve data for the US, Python code used to produce the dataset, some sample homework problems based on the Beveridge curve data, and a link to a web-based tool that students can use to analyze equilibrium in the MP model.

Data

In Figure 3 of our paper, we present the following plot of Beveridge curve data for the US:

The figure is a plot of labor market tightness against the unemployment rate for the US for each month from April 1929 through August 2016. Market tightness is the ratio of job vacancies to unemployed persons. The downward-sloping relationship between market tightness and the unemployment rate is a key prediction of the MP model and this figure provides important empirical justification for the model.
